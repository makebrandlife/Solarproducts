The Latest in Solar Technology – What's Changed in 2025

If you've been following the solar market, you'll know that the last two years have been a leap forward. The buzzwords you'll hear most often now are N-Type TOPCon and bifacial dual-glass modules.

N-Type TOPCon (Tunnel Oxide Passivated Contact)

This is the next step up from traditional PERC cells. N-Type cells resist light-induced degradation, perform better in high temperatures, and deliver higher efficiency — often above 22%. For homeowners, that means more power from the same roof space.

Bifacial Dual-Glass Panels

These panels capture sunlight from both the front and the back. If your roof or terrace reflects light (white paint, tiles, concrete), you can see a 5–25% boost in output. The dual-glass build also makes them tougher and more weather-resistant, with lifespans of 30 years or more.

Half-Cut Cell Design

By splitting cells in half, manufacturers reduce resistive losses and improve shade tolerance. This is now standard in most premium panels.

In short: today's best panels are more efficient, last longer, and perform better in India's hot, humid conditions — especially in coastal states like Kerala.

Government Subsidy – How It Works in 2025

The PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana and state-level rooftop solar schemes continue to offer attractive subsidies for residential installations.

Eligibility

To qualify, your panels must be DCR-compliant (Domestic Content Requirement) — meaning both the cells and modules are made in India. Many WAAREE and Adani models meet this standard.

Subsidy Amounts (for residential rooftop systems)

  • Up to 3 kW: ₹18,000 per kW
  • 3–10 kW: ₹18,000 per kW for first 3 kW, then ₹9,000 per kW for the rest
  • Above 10 kW: Subsidy capped at 10 kW

Why It Matters for Buyers

A DCR-compliant bifacial TOPCon panel might cost a bit more upfront, but the subsidy can offset a large chunk of that cost — making premium tech more affordable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about solar products and technology

What's the difference between a solar kit and a single solar panel?
A solar panel is just one component—it converts sunlight into electricity. A solar kit includes everything you need for a functioning system: panels, inverters, charge controllers, mounting hardware, and sometimes batteries. See our Solar Kits page for full details.
Which solar kit is best for a 2BHK home in India?
For a typical 2BHK home using 8–10 units per day, a 3–5 kW system is ideal. The Luminous NXG+ 1150 or similar kits offer good balance of power and value. Check our Comparison section for detailed analysis.
Do solar panels work during monsoon or cloudy days?
Yes, though at reduced efficiency (around 30–50% of full capacity). Modern panels like the Fujiyama 590W TOPCon perform better in low light than older models. Battery backup is recommended for continuous power.
What does "bifacial" mean in solar panels?
Bifacial panels capture sunlight from both sides, increasing total output by 5–25%. They work best when installed over reflective surfaces (white roofs, gravel, sand). See our Technology section for more on this innovation.
How long do solar batteries last?
Lithium-ion batteries (like in the Star Home kit) last 7–10 years or 3000+ cycles. Lead-acid batteries typically last 3–5 years. Proper maintenance extends lifespan. Read real user experiences in our Testimonials.
Can I run an AC on solar power?
Yes, but you'll need sufficient capacity. A 1.5-ton AC requires about 2 kW of solar panels plus adequate battery storage. The Luminous NXG+ 1100 or larger systems can handle AC loads.
What maintenance do solar panels require?
Minimal maintenance: occasional cleaning (every 2–3 months) with water and soft cloth, checking for shade obstructions, and ensuring connections remain tight. Most systems include monitoring apps. Learn more in our Technology guide.
How much can I save with solar power?
Most homes save 70–90% on electricity bills, with full payback in 4–7 years. Savings depend on your location, consumption, and local electricity rates. See real savings examples in our Testimonials section.
What is the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana subsidy?
This central government scheme offers subsidies up to ₹78,000 for residential rooftop solar installations. Eligibility requires using approved components and installers. Learn more in our Technology section's subsidy breakdown.
Are these products eligible for government subsidies?
Many are, particularly DCR-compliant panels (like certain WAAREE models). Complete kits may qualify if they use approved components. Check our Comparison table for subsidy eligibility notes on each product.
What if a panel gets damaged? Is there a warranty?
Most premium panels come with 25–30 year performance warranties and 10–12 year product warranties. The WAAREE 560W TOPCon, for example, offers 30 years output warranty. Always register your warranty after purchase.
How do I know if my roof is suitable for solar?
Most roofs work if they: 1) receive 4–6 hours of direct sunlight, 2) have adequate structural strength, and 3) aren't heavily shaded. South-facing roofs are ideal in India. Our Solar Kits page has guidance for different roof types.
Can I install solar panels myself?
Kits like the Star Home 250 Watt are designed for DIY installation, but we recommend professional help for grid-tied systems or complex roof mounts. Proper installation ensures safety and maximizes efficiency.
What happens during a power cut with a solar system?
Systems with battery backup automatically switch to solar power during outages. Grid-tied systems without batteries will shut down for safety unless they have special islanding capability. Our Technology section explains these configurations.
How does solar perform in India's extreme heat?
Solar panels actually become slightly less efficient as temperatures rise, but modern panels like WAAREE's TOPCon models handle heat better than older technologies. See our Technology page for heat tolerance comparisons.
What is the lifespan of solar panels?
Most quality panels are warrantied for 25–30 years and can continue producing electricity at reduced capacity beyond that. The Fujiyama 590W panel maintains 87% output even after 30 years.
Are there any hidden costs after buying a solar kit?
The main upfront cost is the kit and installation. Minor ongoing costs may include replacement batteries (in 7–10 years), routine cleaning, and, if grid-tied, minor connection fees. We break down all cost elements in our Detailed Pricing Guide. See user cost breakdowns and tips in Testimonials.
Is installing solar really worth it in 2025 and beyond?
Absolutely, for most Indian homes and small businesses. Falling panel prices, rising grid rates, generous subsidies, and improved technology make solar a smart investment for 2025.
